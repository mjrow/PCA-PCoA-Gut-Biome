# PCA/PCoA Application for Analysis of Gut Microbiomes in Cancer Patients

## 📝 Project Statement
This project aims to demonstrate our understanding of PCA and PCoA, its importance in dimensionality reduction within datasets, and its application to real external datasets. We worked with gut microbiome data collected from cancer patients to demonstrate the usefullness of PCA/PCoA when comparing the gut microbiomes of cancer patients and healthy individuals.

After completing this project we aim to be able to:

1. Explain why high-dimensional biological data often benefits from dimensionality reduction.
2. Standardize and center data, form covariance (or correlation) matrices, and compute principal components.
3. Interpret eigenvalues/eigenvectors in terms of variance explained and biological feature contributions.
4. Explain the difference between **feature-based** methods (PCA) and **distance-based** methods (PCoA), and when each is appropriate.
5. Apply PCA/PCoA to biological datasets (Iris flowers and microbiome/soil samples) and discuss the observed clustering patterns.

## 🏗️ Project Design
**Introduction**:
- Describe how dimensionality reduction is useful and important for analyzing large data sets, especially within areas like microbiology.
- Outline PCA/PCoA and their respective methods and use cases
- Describe how PCA/PCoA can be used for real data sets and how the results can be interpreted
- Introduce the topic of gut microbiomes and the relevance of PCA/PCoA when comparing the gut microbiomes of cancer patients and healthy individuals.
